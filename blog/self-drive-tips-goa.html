🌧️ Monsoon & Seasonal Conditions

Jun-Sept (Monsoon): Roads become slippery. Heavy rainfall causes waterlogging. Visibility reduced. Avoid night driving. Go 40-50 km/h on wet roads.

Oct-May (Dry Season): Best driving conditions. Roads dry, clear visibility. Comfortable speeds possible. Ideal for exploring.

Mar-May (Summer): High heat. Bitumen roads may become soft. Drive carefully, avoid midday driving. Stay hydrated.

⚖️ Traffic Rules & Laws

🚨 Important Rules

•

Keep Left

India drives on LEFT side of road. Different from USA/Europe.

•

Seat Belts MANDATORY

Fine: ₹1,000. Children use child seats if under 4 years.

•

Speed Limits

City: 40 km/h | National Highway: 80 km/h | Regular roads: 60 km/h. Speeding fine: ₹500-2,000.

•

No Honking

Restricted in cities from 10 PM - 6 AM. Fine: ₹100.

•

Drinking & Driving

Zero tolerance. Fine: ₹2,000 first offense, ₹3,000 repeat. Jail possible.

•

Signal Usage

Indicate before turns. Use hazard lights for emergency stops.

🤝 Local Driving Etiquette

✅ Do's

  • ✓ Flash headlights to signal "go ahead"
  • ✓ Always keep right side for fast vehicles
  • ✓ Give way to bigger vehicles (trucks, buses)
  • ✓ Use blinking hazard lights when stopped
  • ✓ Be patient with traffic & pedestrians
  • ✓ Return courtesy waves

❌ Don'ts

  • ✗ Don't tailgate or drive aggressively
  • ✗ Never use cell phone while driving
  • ✗ Don't change lanes abruptly
  • ✗ Avoid honking excessively (unsafe &illegal)
  • ✗ Don't drive under influence
  • ✗ Never leave car unlocked or keys visible

⚠️ Common Road Hazards

🐄 Animals on Roads

Cows, dogs, goats often roam freely. Slow down near villages. Avoid sudden honking (can startle animals).

🏍️ Two-Wheelers & Autos

Motorcycles & auto-rickshaws navigate unpredictably. Always expect sudden movements. Give them space.

🚶 Pedestrians

People may cross roads unexpectedly without looking. Stay alert in markets, residential areas. Slow down near schools.

🌧️ Water Logging

During monsoon, sudden waterlogging on roads. Don't drive through deep water (can cause engine damage).

🕳️ Potholes & Rough Patches

Especially in village roads. Drive slowly, look ahead. Can damage tires or suspension.

🌙 Night Driving

Limited street lights outside cities. Poor visibility. Many unlit vehicles. Avoid night driving if unfamiliar with roads.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best time for self-driving in Goa?

October-May provides best driving conditions. Clear skies, dry roads, good visibility. Avoid June-September monsoon for safety.

Do I need an International Driving Permit?

Recommended for foreign tourists. Get IDP from your home country before travel. Indian license also works for Indian residents.

Is driving challenging for first-timers?

Initial adjustment needed (left-side driving). Start with main highways (good roads). Avoid beaches & citycenters first. After 1-2 days, becomes comfortable.

What should I do if stopped by police?

Stay calm. Produce license, RC, insurance & pollution certificate. Follow instructions. Fines must be paid on-spot through challan. Get receipt. You can contest unfair fines.

How much fuel will I need for Goa?

Maruti Swift does 18-20 km/liter. For 1,000 km trip: 50-60 liters needed = ₹4,000-6,000. Budget ₹350-400 per day for fuel traveling between beaches.
